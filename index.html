Reporting

As a Dental Assistant, you are the eyes and ears of the practice. Reporting concerns is not about getting people in troubleâ€”it is about keeping patients safe. Whether it is a faulty machine, a safety hazard, or a child at risk, you have a professional and legal duty to speak up. If you see something, say something.

The four main reportable areas include:

  • General Breaches: Everyday hazards and infection control.
  • Mandatory Reporting: Protecting vulnerable children.
  • WHS: Ensuring a safe physical environment.
  • Professional Conduct: Managing serious ethical or legal infringements.
